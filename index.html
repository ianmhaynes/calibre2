Our Story

The movement is the heart

For centuries, watchmakers have left their mark inside the case — in the anglage of a plate, the colour of a bridge, the beat of an escapement. Calibre was built to read those marks.

We combine deep horological knowledge with modern AI vision to identify movements from photographs — whether you're a collector evaluating a purchase, a watchmaker researching a service, or simply curious about what lies beneath the dial.

How we identify

Our model is guided by a master horologist's approach: analysing plate architecture, jewel configuration, finishing style, balance wheel design, and the subtle signatures each manufacturer leaves behind.

Uploading multiple images — the movement, caseback, and dial — significantly improves accuracy, particularly for movements that share architecture across manufacturers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the identification?

Accuracy varies by image clarity and the movement's distinctiveness. Common calibres with clear photographs are identified with high confidence. Uploading caseback and dial images significantly improves results. All identifications are for reference purposes only.

What types of movements can you identify?

Mechanical (manual wind and automatic), quartz, and electronic movements from all major manufacturers — ETA, Valjoux, Rolex, Patek Philippe, Jaeger-LeCoultre, Seiko, Citizen, and hundreds more, spanning from the early twentieth century to present day.

Is my first identification really free?

Yes. No account, no credit card. Upload your movement image and receive a full report immediately. After your first identification, each subsequent report is $5, charged securely through Stripe.

What are the similar watch links?

After identification, we suggest three similar watches with links to Google search, so you can explore listings and current market prices for comparable pieces.
